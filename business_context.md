# Business Context Summary

## Industry
Subscription-based B2B services company

---

## Business Model
- Recurring revenue model
- Combination of new customer acquisition and account expansion
- Revenue driven by volume, pricing, and retention dynamics

---

## Current Situation
Over the past 3 quarters:
- Net revenue growth has slowed
- Gross sales appear stable
- Executive concern is rising regarding long-term growth trajectory

Internal debate has emerged around the cause of decline.

---

## Competing Narratives

### Narrative 1: Acquisition Problem
Marketing argues:
- Lead quality has declined
- Market competition has intensified

### Narrative 2: Retention Problem
Customer Success argues:
- Customers are downgrading plans
- Expansion revenue has slowed
- Churn among mid-tenure accounts has increased

### Narrative 3: Pricing Compression
Finance argues:
- ARPA is declining
- Plan mix is shifting downward

Each narrative is plausible.
Each team can produce selective metrics to support its claim.

---

## Why This Matters
The wrong diagnosis will result in:
- Misallocated capital
- Reinforcement of incorrect internal narratives
- Delayed strategic correction

This project examines how the initial framing of the question determines the direction of analysis and ultimately the decision itself.
